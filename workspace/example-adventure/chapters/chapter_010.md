# Chapter 10

Consciousness exploded.

Marcus existed in three places simultaneously. Himself. The woman. The man.

No—not three. One. All merged. All fractured. All screaming.

*Help us help me kill me save us it hurts stop please no more love you always dying living burning can't think can't stop thinking two minds one prison please please please—*

The thoughts crashed over him. Through him. Inside him. No distinction between internal and external. No boundary between Marcus Chen and the lovers trapped for a millennium.

He tried to pull back. Couldn't find the edges of himself. Where did his pattern end and theirs begin?

*Focus.* Evan's teaching. Distant. Fading. *Find the wound. Assess before acting.*

Right. Focus. Marcus existed somewhere in this chaos. His threads were here. New. Bright. Not corrupted yet.

He concentrated on breathing. On his marked palm. On being fifteen-year-old Marcus Chen from Vermont who'd touched a chest three weeks ago and fallen into another world.

The chaos settled . Enough for him to see.

Gods.

The pattern stretched infinitely in all directions. Every moment these two people had experienced together. Every shared heartbeat. Every thought that started in one mind and finished in the other.

A thousand years of enforced intimacy. No privacy. No escape. Total exposure.

Marcus saw everything.

Her name was Lyara. From the Weaver world. Daughter of a scholar, talented in resonance magic. She'd come to the bridge often, studying the Veil, trying to understand how it worked.

His name was Thomas. Earth-born. Descendant of refugees who'd crossed before the Veil sealed. He had resonance too. Enough to reach the bridge from his side.

They'd met by accident. Seen each other through the transparent barrier. Started communicating through touch—palms pressed to opposite sides of the Veil.

Friendship became fascination. Fascination became love.

They'd spent five years meeting at the bridge. Learning each other's languages through gesture and persistence. Sharing their worlds through the barrier.

Until it wasn't enough.

Marcus felt Lyara's desperation. The ache of wanting to touch without glass between them. To hold Thomas properly. To exist in the same space.

She'd found the crystal in her father's collection. Ancient. Forbidden. Designed for emergency fusion magic—binding two dying people to save them both by merging their life forces.

if she modified it, amplified it, directed it across the Veil...

love was enough.

It wasn't.

The fusion hadn't connected them gently. It had ripped them apart and slammed them together. Torn every thread from both patterns and braided them in configurations that violated the fundamental rules of reality.

Physical fusion came first. Their bodies pressed through the Veil, occupying the same space. Bones merged. Organs compressed together. Skin fused. The pain had been unimaginable.

Then mental fusion. Their consciousness forced into a single channel. Every thought visible to the other. No filter. No separation. Identity dissolving into shared awareness.

And finally, magical fusion. Their resonance channels braided together, creating a massive conduit that pulled power from both worlds simultaneously. The energy had nowhere to go. It turned to corruption. Spread outward. Infected everything it touched.

The Veil cracked from the strain. Reality recoiled. And Lyara and Thomas became the wound that wouldn't heal.

Marcus experienced it all. Felt their thousand years of agony compressed into moments. The desperate early years where they tried to separate themselves through force of will. The centuries where they lost hope. The recent decades where madness crept in, eroding what little remained of their individual selves.

Lyara wanted death because she couldn't bear another moment of this violation.

Thomas wanted life because after a thousand years together, separation felt like death anyway.

*Where are the separation points?* Marcus thought through the chaos. *There have to be weak spots. Places where the braid can be undone.*

He pushed deeper. Examined the fusion thread more carefully.

There. At the consciousness layer. A small gap where Lyara's memories of her childhood remained distinct from Thomas's. The fusion hadn't completely overwritten their pasts.

And there. At the physical layer. Their hearts beat together, but they were still two hearts. Still separate organs pressed together, not truly merged.

And there. At the magical layer. A tiny section where their resonance channels ran parallel instead of braided. A brief moment where they'd both channeled identically and the braid loosened.

Separation points. Small. Fragile. But real.

If Marcus could widen those gaps. Reinforce the distinct sections. Carefully unbraid the forced connections...

It would take forever. The fusion was too extensive. Too deep.

Unless.

Marcus remembered what Evan said. *You don't heal everything at once. You find the source and cut it.*

The source wasn't the current fusion. It was the original binding.

The moment Lyara activated the crystal. The instant the forced connection began.

If Marcus could find that moment in the pattern. That single point where everything went wrong. And if he could sever it—cut the binding at its origin—then the thousand years of fusion might unravel on its own.

But severing a binding that old would create a massive discharge. All that trapped energy releasing at once. It could destroy the bridge. Collapse the Veil entirely.

Or it could work. Release the pressure. Let reality snap back into proper configuration.

Fifty-fifty chance. Maybe worse.

The Veil shuddered. Even from inside the pattern, Marcus felt it. Reality buckling under the weight of corruption.

He didn't have time for a better plan.

Marcus dove backward through the pattern. Through a thousand years of suffering. Through centuries of despair. Through decades of desperate hope.

Back to that moment.

Lyara standing on the bridge. Crystal pulsing in her hands. Thomas across from her, reaching, not understanding what she was about to do.

She spoke the activation words. Old magic. Dangerous magic.

The crystal blazed.

Marcus saw it clearly now. The binding initiated at the exact center point between them. A thread of pure violet energy punching through the Veil, grabbing both their patterns simultaneously.

The thread was still there. Still active. The foundation supporting a thousand years of fusion.

Cut it.

Marcus manifested his blade. His sharpest edge. The combat form he'd learned reluctantly in training.

But here, in the pattern, it wasn't violent. It was surgical. Precise.

He brought the blade down on the violet thread.

It resisted. Old magic reinforced by a millennium of accumulated power. Marcus's first strike barely scratched it.

He struck again. Channeled more energy. The blade burned brighter.

The thread frayed.

*NO!* Lyara's consciousness screamed. *You'll kill us both! The discharge will—*

*Let him try!* Thomas's consciousness overlapped hers. *We're dying anyway. At least this way we choose—*

Marcus struck a third time. Put everything he had into it. Every ounce of channeling capacity. Every technique Evan taught him. Every desperate hope that this impossible plan might work.

The violet thread snapped.

Reality convulsed.

The discharge hit Marcus like a physical blow. Violet energy exploded outward, tearing through the pattern. the fusion beginning to unravel. Threads separating. Braids loosening.

But too fast. Too violent. The separation was ripping Lyara and Thomas apart with the same force that had fused them.

*No no no—* Marcus tried to slow it. Channel the discharge. Redirect the energy.

He wrapped his own pattern around theirs. Used his golden light as a buffer. Absorbed as much of the violent separation as he could.

Pain tore through him. His channels screamed. The corruption tried to infect him, find purchase in his pattern.

But Marcus held firm. *You don't die. Not after surviving this long. I won't let you.*

He guided the separation. Made it gradual instead of explosive. Consciousness first—giving them back their individual minds. Then magical—separating their resonance channels carefully. Finally physical—letting their bodies pull apart without tearing.

The process took seconds that felt like hours. Marcus's awareness dimmed. His channels burned. his own threads starting to fray from the strain.

*Almost there. Hold on.*

One final push. Marcus severed the last connection. Let the discharge flow through him and out. Channeled it away from the lovers, away from the Veil, away from anything vulnerable.

The energy dissipated into the void between realities.

Silence.

Marcus opened eyes he didn't remember closing.

He lay on the bridge, gasping. His marked palm throbbed with agony. His channels felt like scorched earth.

Twenty feet away, two people lay separated.

Lyara. Dark hair spread around her head. Eyes closed. Breathing.

Thomas. Light hair plastered to his forehead with sweat. Eyes closed. Breathing.

Both breathing.

Alive.

Separate.

Marcus tried to sit up. Couldn't. His body refused to respond. He'd pushed too far. Channeled too much.

But he'd done it. Separated them. Saved them.

Above, the Veil pulsed. The cracks were sealing. Without the corruption source constantly feeding them, reality was healing itself.

Marcus smiled.

Then darkness took him.
