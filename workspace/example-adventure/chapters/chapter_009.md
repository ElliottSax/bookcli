# Chapter 9

The bridge hummed beneath Marcus's feet.

Not sound exactly. Vibration. Like the entire structure  with some frequency below hearing. Each step sent tingles through his boots, up his legs, settling in his marked palm.

The corrupted figures grew larger as he approached. What had seemed like distant shapes resolved into horrifying detail.

Two people. Or what remained of them.

A man and a woman, Marcus thought, though it was hard to tell. The fusion had twisted them. Their joined hands had merged into a single mass of flesh and bone—three arms total where there should have been four. One from him, one from her, and a third abomination sprouting from the fusion point.

Their chests pressed together, ribs visible through translucent skin. Hearts beating in synchronization, one rhythm for two bodies. Or one body made from two.

And their heads. Gods, their heads.

Foreheads fused. Eyes open—two facing one direction, two facing the other. Mouths moving independently, forming words without sound. Constant, silent screaming.

Black tendrils wrapped around them like vines. No—not wrapped. Growing from them. The corruption wasn't external. It was internal, bursting through their skin in thick cables that spread across the bridge, climbed the Veil, infected everything they touched.

Marcus stopped twenty feet away.

His stomach churned. His hands shook. This wasn't a tangle. It was a nightmare. A thousand-year torture sustained by magic that should have let them die.

How was he supposed to fix this?

*Assess first,* Evan's voice echoed in his memory. *Find the wound. Understand the pattern. Never cut without seeing the full picture.*

Marcus took a breath. Centered himself. Extended his awareness outward.

The world shifted.

Threads appeared. Not the simple practice patterns from Evan's fragments. Not Aria's localized scar tissue.

Millions of threads. Billions. Every choice these two people had made in a thousand years. Every thought. Every moment of agony. All woven together in a mass so dense it looked solid.

Gold and silver threads corrupted to sickly green. Crimson threads turned black. And everywhere, everywhere, the infection spreading through the weave like oil through water.

Marcus pushed deeper. Followed the corruption backward. Traced it to its source.

There. At the center. Where their foreheads fused.

A single connection. Forced. Wrong. A thread that should never have existed, binding two patterns that were never meant to merge.

Someone had done this deliberately. Taken two separate people and forced their threads together with magic so powerful it had scarred reality itself.

*Why?* Marcus thought. *Why would anyone do this?*

He pushed closer. Tried to read the thread. Understand its purpose.

Pain slammed into him.

Not his pain. Theirs.

A thousand years of suffering compressed into a single instant. Burning. Stretching. Being pulled apart and forced together simultaneously. Consciousness fractured across two minds that couldn't separate. Thoughts that began in one brain and finished in another. Identity dissolving into shared madness.

Marcus gasped. Pulled back. His channels screamed from the feedback.

But he'd seen something. Before the pain forced him out.

A memory. Embedded in the thread.

A woman standing on this bridge. Young, maybe twenty. Dark hair. Determined eyes. She held something in her hands—a crystal pulsing with violet light.

On the opposite side of the bridge, a man. Same age. Light hair. Desperate expression. Reaching toward her.

Between them, the Veil. Whole. Unbroken. Separating them completely.

The woman spoke words Marcus couldn't hear. Raised the crystal. Light blazed from it, so bright it burned the memory white.

When the light faded, they were fused. Screaming. Corruption spreading from the fusion point. The Veil cracking as their combined agony tore through reality.

They'd been lovers. Separated by the Veil. One from each world.

She'd tried to bridge the gap. Create a permanent connection that would let them be together.

It had destroyed them both.

Marcus opened his eyes. The real figures stood before him, still trapped in that moment. Still paying for one woman's desperate attempt to defy the separation.

"I'm sorry," he whispered.

The woman's eyes—the two facing him—moved. Focused on his face.

Marcus froze.

She was still in there. Still conscious. After a thousand years of this, awareness remained.

Her mouth moved. Forming words. No sound came out, but Marcus could read her lips.

*Kill me.*

Then the man's eyes found him. The other two eyes, facing the opposite direction, swiveling in their sockets to see.

His mouth moved too.

*Save us.*

Two different requests. Two different people trapped in one body. One wanting death. One clinging to hope.

Marcus's marked palm burned.

He had the power to grant either wish. Cut the thread. End their suffering permanently. Or try to separate them. Give them a chance at life—if it was even possible after a millennium of fusion.

But Kira's words echoed in his mind.

*If you save the Veil, the Weavers won't let you go. Mission after mission. Tangle after tangle. Until you burn out or break.*

And Evan's warning.

*Trying to heal this all at once would kill you. You'd die before making progress.*

And the Elder's cold telepathic voice.

*The method is irrelevant. Only results matter.*

Marcus looked at them. At the corruption spreading from their bodies. At the cracks in the Veil widening with each passing moment. At the two realities pressing against the failing barrier.

Millions would die if he failed.

Billions.

The quick solution was obvious. Cut the central thread. Kill them both. Let the Veil seal itself without the corruption source.

But that wasn't why he'd learned Evan's techniques. Wasn't why he'd spent every night studying pattern manipulation while the other keys slept. Wasn't why he'd taught his friends to look for alternatives.

Marcus stepped closer.

The corruption's black tendrils sensed him. Reached for him. Hungry.

He channeled. Golden light manifested around his hands—not his combat form. His illumination. The gentle light that showed truth.

The tendrils recoiled.

"I'm going to try to help you," Marcus said. To both of them. To the woman who wanted death and the man who wanted life. "I don't know if I can separate you. But I'm going to try."

The woman's eyes filled with tears. Or maybe they were the man's tears. Hard to tell when they shared everything.

Marcus placed his hands on the bridge floor. Closed his eyes. Extended his awareness fully into the pattern.

The tangle's full scope revealed itself.

Gods. It was so much worse than he'd thought.

The original fusion thread had spread corruption to every other connection these two had. Family. Friends. Memories of people they'd known before the fusion. All infected. All turned to poison.

And those infected threads had spread further. Touched other patterns. Created secondary tangles. Tertiary ones. A thousand-year cascade of corruption spreading through both realities like cancer.

This wasn't one tangle. It was a million tangles. All connected. All feeding the central wound.

If Marcus cut the source, all those secondary tangles would collapse. Might even heal on their own.

But could he separate the source without killing them?

He traced the fusion thread again. Followed it to where it connected their foreheads.

The thread wasn't single. It was braided. Hundreds of forced connections woven together. Consciousness. Memory. Physical fusion. Magical binding. Each strand would need to be separated individually.

Even attempting that would take hours. Days, maybe.

Marcus didn't have days. He had minutes before the Veil collapsed completely.

He pulled back. Opened his eyes.

The woman's mouth moved again. *Please. No more.*

The man's mouth formed different words. *Don't give up.*

Two people. One prison. Both suffering.

Marcus's hands clenched.

There was one possibility. One technique Evan had mentioned in passing but never demonstrated.

Shared consciousness protocol. Instead of separating from the outside, Marcus could join them. Enter the fusion. Experience their pattern from within. From that perspective, he might be able to find separation points he couldn't see externally.

But joining a corruption this deep risked permanent infection. If Marcus couldn't pull free, he'd become part of the tangle. Another victim instead of a healer.

The Veil shuddered. A massive crack split across its surface. Corruption poured through like black rain.

Time was up.

Marcus had to choose.

The quick way. Cut and run. Save millions by killing two.

Or the impossible way. Risk everything for the chance—slim as it was—of saving them all.

His marked palm throbbed in rhythm with the bridge's vibration.

*You are a weapon,* the Weaver had told him. *Sharp weapons accomplish their purpose cleanly.*

But Marcus wasn't a weapon.

He was a key. A bridge. Someone who could go where Weavers couldn't. See what they couldn't perceive.

Maybe that meant something more than killing efficiently.

Marcus took a breath.

Stepped forward.

Placed both hands on the fused figures.

And dove into the pattern.
