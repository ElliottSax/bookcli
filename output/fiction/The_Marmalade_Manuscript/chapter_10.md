Inside, the world had shrunk to the circle of lamplight pooling on the worn kitchen table, where Eleanor Vance sat opposite Thomas Bell. The air was thick with the scent of damp wool, old wood, and the ghost of Agatha's lavender polish. The soft, golden glow of the lamp cast a warm, comforting light on the lines etched into Thomas's face, but it couldn't penetrate the shadows that had haunted him for fifty years.

Thomas's large, work-roughened hands were wrapped around a mug of tea Eleanor Vance had made, but he hadn't taken a sip. He simply stared into its amber depths as if reading leaves at the bottom of a well. The steam rising from the tea danced in the air, carrying the faint scent of Earl Grey, a comforting aroma that seemed to match the soothing rhythm of the rain. The silence wasn't empty; it was full of the weight of Thomas's secrets, the burden he had carried for so long.

Eleanor Vance waited. The silence wasn't oppressive; it was a heavy, comforting blanket that wrapped around her, a reminder that she was there for Thomas, that he could trust her.
