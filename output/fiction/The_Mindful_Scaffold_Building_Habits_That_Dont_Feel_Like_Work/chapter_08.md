The Story of the Self

We all have a story about who we are, a narrative made up of our actions, comments from others, our own judgments, and the roles we play. We might tell ourselves, "I'm terrible with mornings," "I'm not creative," "I'm someone who gives up," or "I'm disorganized." These aren't facts; they're conclusions we've repeated until they feel true.
Each time you mindfully performed your micro-habit, you added a contradictory sentence to that old story. At first, it was a bold statement: "Today, I did my one push-up!" The old narrative dismissed it as a fluke. But you persisted, not forcing it, but rather observing it with curiosity. You used your mindfulness to witness the old story and then gently focus on the new action. With each repetition, the new sentence became more confident. The bold statement became a simple fact, and now it's starting to blend with your sense of identity.
