Chapter 9: Scaffolding for Focus and Flow

She sat at her desk with a fresh cup cooling beside her, her laptop open to a blank page. The cursor blinked, a tiny, steady pulse that marked the waiting work. She had the time, the quiet, the intention. But her mind was like a bird trapped indoors, flitting from an unpaid bill on the desk, to a worry about an email she’d sent, to a sudden urge to look up how long backyard squirrels live.
Maya sighed, the sound loud in the quiet room. This was supposed to be her time for deep work, when she could lose herself in writing and the outside world would fade away. Instead, her mind was a restless animal, pacing in its cage. The pressure of deadlines, the flood of emails, the nagging feeling she wasn’t good enough—it all pressed down on her from the inside.
