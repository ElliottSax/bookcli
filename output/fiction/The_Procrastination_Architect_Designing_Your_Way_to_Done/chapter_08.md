The Power of the “Not Now” List

The Sculptor’s favorite tactic is to convince you that every thought, every idea, every potential improvement is urgent. It isn’t. The “Not Now” List is your defense.

During a work sprint, if a thought arises that is not directly related to the task at hand, you write it down. Physically. On paper. The act of writing is a ritualized surrender. You are acknowledging the thought, but you are also saying, “This is not the time for you. You will have your turn.”

The list might look like this:
Research better brick suppliers (for the review phase)
Ask the client about the color palette (for the next meeting)
Reorganize the tool shed (for the weekend)

The list is not a to-do list. It’s a holding cell for distractions. It’s a promise to the Sculptor that her ideas will be heard, but only when the fortress is down.

The Sculptor will try to argue. “But this is important!” You must remain calm. “I hear you. And I will address it. But not now.” The key is to say it with conviction, not with resignation. You are the project manager. You are in control.
