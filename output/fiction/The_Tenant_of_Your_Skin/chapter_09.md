Leo's hand, wrapped around the familiar weight of the fountain pen, hovered above it. The air in the study was thick, tasting of dust, old paper, and the coppery tang of impending rain. But there was another taste, too, one only he could perceive: the cloying sweetness of decayed roses, Thorne's psychic scent, now woven into the very fabric of his own breath.
