Vance’s shoulders slumped, the performance ending not with a bang, but a silent, profound deflation. The police would come. The story would unravel. But in that quiet, something else happened. He had no plan, no script. For the first time in years, his next move was his own. He turned his face away from the glittering skyline of the city he’d helped cage, and walked toward the quieter streets, where ordinary lives, messy and unobserved, carried on in the dark. He had broken the system. Now, he had to learn how to live in the world he’d helped set free. The work was just beginning.
