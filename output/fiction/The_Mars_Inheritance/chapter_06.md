# Chapter 6: The Hidden Laboratory

As they pushed further into the Martian terrain, the rugged landscape stretched out like an endless red plain. Captain Jaxon’s mind kept returning to the cryptic message from the unknown sender. The words “Erebus Initiative” and “Project Nova” repeated in his thoughts, impossible to ignore. He had shown the message to Dr. Patel and Maya, but none of them could figure out what it meant or how it linked to the Mars Inheritance.
Their search through the old research sites had turned up almost nothing, and the crew was growing impatient. The constant red dunes, jagged rocks, and heavy silence were wearing on everyone. Jaxon knew they needed a real lead—something to pull the team back together.
"Captain, I’m picking up strange energy readings up ahead," said Ensign Amy K’Rhyn, her voice bright with interest as she monitored the rover’s sensors. She leaned toward the console, focused on the incoming data.
Jaxon’s attention snapped back. He studied the screen. "Let’s check it out. Change course, heading two-seven-zero." A quick rush of energy went through him as he gave the order.
The rover turned slowly, tires churning up rust-colored dust. The view shifted, and in the distance a group of odd, dome-shaped structures came into sight. They looked like habitats or labs, half-buried in the Martian ground. The team traded hopeful looks, their tiredness fading for a moment.
As they got closer, Jaxon saw the domes were enclosed by a high wall topped with razor wire. The entrance was a heavy airlock, guarded by two threatening turrets. The team’s excitement turned to wariness.
"This isn’t any normal research site," Maya said, her expression concerned. "What were they doing here that needed so much security?"

"Maybe it wasn’t research at all," Dr. Patel offered, her eyes alive with interest. "Maybe it was something secret."

Jaxon’s instinct told him this was important. He made the call, voice steady. "We’re going in. Amy, can you get through the airlock security?"

Ensign K’Rhyn was already working. "Trying now, Captain. But the encryption is strong. This could take a few minutes."

The team waited, tension building, while Amy worked. Time appeared to slow. Then, with a low hum, the outer airlock door slid open, revealing a shadowy chamber inside. They shared a look of nervous anticipation.
After confirming their suit systems and readying their rifles, they stepped through. The airlock sealed behind them. Pressure shifted slightly, and the inner door opened. They moved forward carefully, scanning the corridor.
