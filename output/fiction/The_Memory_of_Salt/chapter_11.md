Chapter 11: Rooftop Showdown

The rooftop of the Grand Imperial Hotel was a forgotten kingdom. A vast, tar-painted plain littered with the skeletal remains of old antennae, the ghostly shapes of laundry lines, and the hulking, wheezing forms of ventilation units. Beyond the low parapet, the city of Valois sprawled like a dying beast glittering in its own sweat. Gas lamps and new electric signs bled their colours into the foggy night, painting the undersides of the clouds in hues of jaundiced yellow and sickly green. The air tasted of coal smoke, distant brine, and the high, thin scent of impending rain.

Elara stood at the centre of this desolate stage, the forged formula—a delicate vial of amber-tinted water and essential oils—cold in her clenched fist. Opposite her, The Chemist was a study in ruined elegance. His velvet jacket was frayed at the cuffs, his posture a desperate attempt to maintain a dignity his eyes had long since surrendered. Lin was beside him, a pale statue, the muzzle of The Chemist’s small, pearl-handled revolver a dark star against her temple.

“The formula, Miss Vance,” The Chemist repeated, his voice a rasp. “Place it on the vent housing and step back. Do not think me so lost I cannot recognise a bluff.”

“It’s not a bluff,” Elara said. Her own voice sounded strange to her, calm and hollow, like a bell rung in an empty room. Her mind was a split screen: one half tracking the minute tremble in The Chemist’s gun hand, the scent of Lin’s fear-sweat (jasmine and salt), the distant wail of a siren somewhere in the city’s bowels. The other half was in the past, watching Sir Arthur’s surprised face as the dagger went in, feeling the ghost of that panic, so human it was almost pitiable. Almost.
