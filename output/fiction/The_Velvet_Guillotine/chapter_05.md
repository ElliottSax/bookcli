The rain had stopped, leaving the city steaming like a freshly brewed cup of coffee, the steam rising from the wet cobblestone streets to cling to the ancient buildings that appeared to lean in, whispering secrets to one another. From the service alley behind the mansion on Rue de l'Observance, Léo watched the guests arrive, their black cars emerging like sleek, dark falcons, their passengers spilling out onto the pavement with the bandwidth of champagne and diamonds.
As they stepped out of the vehicles, their laughter peeled off the night air, shards of crystal that sliced through the mist, their faces alight with the fermented glint of the party already begun. The air was riotous, filling with the sweet sizzle of canapés, the preciously judgmental wisps of expensive perfume, and the faint, clammy stench of the courtyard restaurant nearby.
Léo thumbed the starched collar of his waiter's uniform, the sharp edge alignment gauging his discomfort. It was a just-mildly-awkward pressure, not enough to disturb the minuscule earnest haziness in the back of his mind. Beside him, Felix slid a frictionless glance toward the crowd.
