It was parked in a derelict lot on the industrial edge of the city, where the cobblestones gave way to cracked asphalt and the scent of the river was a cold, metallic rot. The air clung to her skin like a damp shroud, weighing her down with every step. The only sound was the soft patter of raindrops on the pavement, a relentless beat that seemed to match the thrumming of her own heart. Elara Vance's breath fogged before her as she approached, each step feeling irrevocable. The door was unlocked, a small, inviting crack in the otherwise imposing bulk of the truck.

As she pushed the door open, a warm, golden light spilled out, bathing her in its radiance. The interior of the truck was a capsule of focused heat and startling order. Unlike the chaotic, living abundance of Simone Thorne's greenhouse, this was a surgeon's theatre. Every utensil hung from a magnetic bar, gleaming in the soft light. Ingredients were in stainless steel containers, labelled with precise tape. The labels themselves were a marvel of attention to detail, each one proof of Gwen's meticulous nature. And at the centre of it all, her back to the door, stood Gwen. She was whisking a bowl of batter, her movements economical, hypnotic. The motion was almost meditative, a soothing balm for the soul.

The sound of sizzling butter and the sweet, cloying scent of sugar wafted through the air, a heady mix of sweet and savory that made Elara Vance's stomach growl with anticipation. Her mouth watered, and her senses came alive, drinking in the aromas and sounds of the kitchen. The air was thick with the smell of browned butter, vanilla pod, and beneath it, a faint, floral note she couldn't place—not the cloying perfume of Simone Thorne's False Heart's Ease, but something deeper, more melancholy.

Gwen didn't turn, her attention fixed on the task at hand. Elara Vance felt a pang of disappointment, a sense of loss that she couldn't quite explain. She had been looking forward to this moment for so long, and now that it was here, she felt...underwhelmed. The silence between them was a palpable thing, a living, breathing entity that seemed to pulse with its own rhythm.

As she waited, Elara Vance's eyes roamed the kitchen, taking in the precision of Gwen's workspace. The stainless steel countertops were spotless, the utensils gleaming in the soft light. The air was warm and heavy, thick with the scent of cooking oil and the promise of something delicious. Elara Vance's stomach growled, and she felt a pang of hunger, a sense of longing that she couldn't quite explain.
